[1] Congolese rebels have taken their two-month campaign to oust President Laurent Kabila to the Internet. [2] After a day of fighting, Congolese rebels said Sunday they had entered Kindu, the strategic town and airbase in eastern Congo used by the government to halt their advances. [3] On Sunday, a spokesman in Goma boasted that Kindu was already under rebel control. [4] The rebels have targeted Kindu since late August, when they were forced to retreat from the outskirts of Kinshasa after Angola and Zimbabwe sent troops to bail out Kabila. [5] On Saturday, the rebels shot down a Congolese Boeing 727 over the Kindu airport. [6] Rebels said the airplane was carrying 40 troops and ammunition.
