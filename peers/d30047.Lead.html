[1] Two astronauts ventured back out on another spacewalk Wednesday to attach antennas to the international space station under construction nearly 250 miles above Earth. [2] For the second time this week, Jerry Ross and James Newman floated out the hatch of the shuttle Endeavour to work on the seven-story, 35-ton station taking shape in the open cargo bay. [3] Their No. 1 priority was to attach two 100-pound antennas to Unity, the American-made side of the space station. [4] Their other job, time permitting, was trickier: trying to open a stuck antenna on Zarya, the Russian-built control module.
