[1] In tight congressional and Senate races leading up to the Nov. [2] 1998 elections, strategists and candidates were unsure how to deal with the Clinton/Lewinsky scandal. [3] Though the President pointed to his record while stumping, many Democrats, embarrassed by his behavior, distanced themselves from him. [4] With impeachment a possibility, the Senate races assumed even greater importance. [5] While it was unlikely the Republicans would win enough Senate seats to remove the President, Democrats feared their opponents could gain a filibuster-proof, 60-seat majority. [6] The NY senate race between Republican D'Amato and Schumer was one of the tightest in the nation.
