[1] China continues to arrest dissidents even as it prepares to sign a UN human rights treaty and to host the first visit by a British PM in seven years. [2] The dissident China Democracy Party believes it is legal to challenge the communists' power monopoly, but the PRC cites a "grab-bag" section of the criminal code making almost any political activity illegal. [3] The US has protested the arrests. [4] Two dissidents, Qin Yongmin and Wang Youcai must defend themselves at trail since lawyers have been dissuaded from assisting them. [5] Other dissidents continue to be harassed and arrested. [6] One dissident, Yao Zhenxian, was released from a labor camp and fled to New York.
