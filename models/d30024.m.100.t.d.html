[1] Most Democrats, except for former Clinton strategist James Carville, were hesitant to attack Speaker Newt Gingrich as the 1998 elections approached. [2] Republicans were expected to add to their majority in Congress. [3] However, Republicans lost seats in both the House and Senate. [4] Republicans became disenchanted with the Gingrich "vision" and blamed him for the losses. [5] An active campaign began to replace Gingrich as Speaker with Louisiana Congressman Robert Livingston as the main actor. [6] After a losing struggle to maintain his position, Gingrich resigned as Speaker. [7] House Republicans then turned their attention to impeachment proceedings of President Clinton.
