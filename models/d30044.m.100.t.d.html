[1] An international incident resulted after the arrest in Rome of Abdullah Ocalan, the leader of the Kurdistan Workers Party (PKK). [2] An armed struggle has ensued since the PKK was formed in 1978, and nearly 37,000 died. [3] Turkey wants Ocalan extradited but Italy is reluctant since Turkey still has the death penalty. [4] Kurds from all over Europe have come to Rome, or at least tried to, to protest Ocalan's detention and to urge asylum for him. [5] Turkey has said countries bordering eastern Turkey have harbored Kurdish rebels and Greece has voiced support for the Kurds. [6] Prisoners in Turkey held an Italian inmate hostage in hope of forcing Italy to extradite Ocalan.
