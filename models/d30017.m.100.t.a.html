[1] Famine had become the rule as North Korea entered its fourth winter of chronic food shortage in 1998. [2] In 1997 the nation produced only 2.8 million tons of grain of the 4.5 million required to feed its 23 million people. [3] Poor harvests, floods and droughts contributed to the problem exasperated by government restrictions on international aid. [4] Two million may have died from famine and the U.N. reported that 65% of the children under 7 showed stunted growth. [5] One report concluded that famine and a failed public health system had produced a generation of physically and mentally impaired children. [6] Similar conditions were said to be developing in Cambodia.
