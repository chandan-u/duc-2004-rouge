[1] President Boris Yeltsin's health has become a matter of great concern to the Russian leadership. [2] The concern began in 1996 when he had a heart attack followed by bypass surgery. [3] Illness has often sidelined him during his seven years in power. [4] He recently cut short a trip to Central Asia because of a respiratory infection and he later canceled two out-of-country summits. [5] This revived questions about his ability to lead Russia through any crisis. [6] Yeltsin refuses to admit he is seriously ill and his condition is kept secret, even the cause for burns on his hands. [7] Russia's leaders are calling for his resignation and question his legal right to seek reelection.
