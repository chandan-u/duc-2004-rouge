[1] The future of the former Portuguese island colony East Timor is in doubt. [2] Indonesia invaded it in 1975 and later annexed it, but not recognized by the UN. [3] East Timor's spiritual leader, Bishop Carlos Belok the 1996 Nobel Peace laureate, has reported killings by Indonesian troops. [4] Portugal has accused Indonesia of failing to reduce its military presence in East Timor. [5] Australia and Taiwan are reluctant to antagonize Indonesia on this issue. [6] Violence has also occurred in Jakarta and west Timor. [7] The UN has a plan for East Timor and Indonesia is offering a small measure of autonomy, but few observers express hope for a favorable outcome for the talks.
