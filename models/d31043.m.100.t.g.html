[1] Lebanon's leadership changed in Nov 1998. [2] Army commander Emile Lahoud was elected to a 6-year term as President by the Parliament and took office on Nov 24. [3] He had the backing of Syrian President Assad, the powerbroker in Lebanon, and a special constitutional amendment in Oct cleared his way. [4] He did not immediately ask Prime Minister Rafik Hariri to form a new government. [5] Hariri, the nation's top businessman, had served three terms and rebuilt the nation, but some accused him of corruption. [6] His support in the Parliament had also slipped, but many believed Lahoud was trying to assert his authority. [7] When finally asked to form a government, Hariri refused.
