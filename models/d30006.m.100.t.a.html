[1] In a dispute over a new collective bargaining agreement the National Basketball Association owners declared a lockout on July 1, 1998. [2] They wanted to discard a clause in the old agreement allowing teams to pay their own free agents whatever they wanted, substituting a hard salary cap. [3] The players wanted to keep earning as much as possible. [4] On Oct. [5] 5 all 114 preseason games were cancelled. [6] The players then proposed a 50% tax on salaries above $18 million that the owners rejected. [7] On Oct. [8] 13 the NBA cancelled the first two weeks of the regular season. [9] By Oct. [10] 21 the entire season seemed in jeopardy in the interests of the best paid.
