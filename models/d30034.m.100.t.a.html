[1] Indonesia invaded the former Portuguese territory of East Timor in 1975 and annexed it in 1976. [2] By late 1998 while East Timorese called for independence and accused Indonesian troops of yet another massacre of civilians, Portugal cut off talks with Indonesia. [3] Internationally, Taiwan was timid fearing antagonizing Indonesia, Australians protested against training Indonesian military who might be assigned to East Timor, and fifteen European Union leaders endorsed Portugal's call for a referendum on East Timor's future. [4] A U.N. enjoy saw a peaceful solution as distant, but sensed a "newfound taste for compromise."
