[1] In an effort to stem the financial crisis in Brazil, the world's ninth largest economy and the financial engine of Latin America, U.S. officials have signaled a willingness to provide billions in direct aid. [2] Brazil is also negotiating with the IMF for a $30 billion bail-out package. [3] The agreement almost certainly includes unpopular austerity measures to trim the country's budget deficit.
