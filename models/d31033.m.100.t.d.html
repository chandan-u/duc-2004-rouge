[1] The government suit against Microsoft is the most aggressive antitrust case in a quarter century. [2] The heart of the case is the Internet browser battle between Microsoft and Netscape. [3] Microsoft says that its Internet Explorer is an integral part of its Windows system, the industry dominant operating system. [4] Microsoft, it is argued, has told computer manufacturers that if they want Windows, they must forgo Netscape. [5] The Justice Department and 20 states are joined in the action brought under the Sherman Antitrust Act. [6] Microsoft's chairman, Bill Gates, usually seen as a visionary is portrayed in much darker tones in the trial.
