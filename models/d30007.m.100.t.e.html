[1] Civil strife, tribal rivalry and rebellion has given Congolese rebels control of 40 percent of Congo. [2] The rebels are ethnic Tutsis, disenchanted members of President Kabila's army and opposition politicians. [3] They are accused of being puppets to Uganda and Rwanda. [4] They launched an attack in Kindu, the strategic town and airbase in eastern Congo used by government to halt their advances. [5] Rebels downed a jetliner carrying 40 people from Kindu but fighting has subsided. [6] They have taken their two-month campaign to the Internet to tell their side of the story. [7] Kabila, refusing to negotiate with them, is accused of mismanagement and causing tribal divisiveness.
