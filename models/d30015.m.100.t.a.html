[3] While supressing the Albanian independence movement they had massacred hundreds of civilians and left 275,000+ refugees. [4] NATO threatened airstrikes unless hostilities ceased and peace talks began. [5] U.S. envoy Holbrooke insisted that Milosevic pull all forces out of Kosovo. [6] Milosevic called the NATO threat "a criminal act" and said Holbrooke aided Albanian terrorists. [7] Russia also urged an end to hostilities, but the Yugoslavs denied any fighting and vowed to defend their country if attacked. [8] Neither side budged as NATO seemed to wait for a U.N. decision.
