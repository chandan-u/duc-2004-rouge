[1] Although China was expected to sign a key U.N. human rights treaty, it continues to question and arrest political dissidents. [2] Qin Yongmin was arrested following his attempt to form a human rights monitoring group. [3] Fellow dissident Xu Wenli was arrested for trying to form an opposition party. [4] Another dissident flew China for the U.S. to avoid arrest. [5] In making the arrests, China has charged that the dissidents have threatened national security. [6] Facing trial, Xu Wenli and Qin Yongmin were unable to obtain legal counsel. [7] Qin Youngmin, attempting to defend himself, was cut off several times by the judge in a trial lasting just over two hours.
