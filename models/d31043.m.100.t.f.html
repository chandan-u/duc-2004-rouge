[1] Army Commander and Maronite Catholic Emile Lahoud was named Lebanon's president for the next 6 years, chosen by outgoing President Hrawi and Syria's Hafez Assad. [2] Traditionally the president is a Maronite, the PM a Sunni, and Speaker of Parliament a Shiite. [3] Election by Parliament is a formality. [4] The constitution was amended to permit senior public servants to be president. [5] Lahoud rebuilt the army and ended militia reign. [6] Walid Jumblatt opposed a military president, fearing a return of surveillance on civilians. [7] PM Rafik Hariri, a businessman who rebuilt the country and economy, refused his delayed re-appointment by Lahoud, who vowed to fight corruption.
