[1] On Oct. [2] 16, 1998 British police arrested former Chilean dictator Pinochet on a Spanish warrant charging murder of Spaniards in Chile, 1973-1983. [3] Fidel Castro denounced the arrest. [4] The Chilean government protested strongly. [5] While the British government defended the arrest, it and the Spanish government took no stand on extradition of Pinochet to Spain, leaving it to the courts. [6] Chilean legislators lobbied in Madrid against extradition, while others endorsed it. [7] Then new charges were filed for crimes against Swiss and French citizens. [8] Pinochet's wife and family pleaded that he was too sick to face extradition. [9] As of Oct. [10] 28 the matter was not resolved.
