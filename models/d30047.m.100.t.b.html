[1] NASA and the Russian Space Agency readied the first part of an international space station for launch from Baikonur base. [2] They decided not to change the orbit of the Zarya module as Russia had requested. [3] Zarya's Nov. [4] 20 launch was a success. [5] The Zarya module orbited Earth for 2 weeks before a rendezvous with U.S. shuttle Endeavor to join it to the Unity module. [6] It was tweaked and working well. [7] It will serve as a space tugboat. [8] The first 2 building blocks of the international space station were successfully joined by Endeavor astronauts in the shuttle's open cargo bay. [9] Endeavor astronauts made 2 of 3 planned spacewalks to work on the space station modules.
