[1] Eleven countries were to adopt a common European currency, the euro, on Dec. [2] 31, 1998. [3] In November and December there were various reactions. [4] France made moves toward a pan-European equity market. [5] Ten of the countries quickly cut interest rates causing fear of overheating in some economies. [6] In Denmark, which had earlier rejected the euro, a majority was now in favor. [7] And in faraway China, the euro was permitted in financial exchanges. [8] Whatever the outcome, the euro's birthday, Dec. [9] 31, 1998, would be an historical date. [10] Some saw it as a step towards political union while others already considered themselves as citizens of Europe.
