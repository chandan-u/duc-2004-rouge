[1] Abdullah Ocalan, the leader of the Kurdish Workers Party, was detained in the Rome airport and is asking for political asylum. [2] Ocalan has led the Kurdish insurgents in southeastern Turkey since 1978. [3] Turkey, where he faces the death penalty, immediately requested his extradition and has heavily pressured Italy. [4] Germany also wants him. [5] Greeks and leftist Italians oppose his extradition. [6] Others in Europe have joined with Kurds in staging demonstrations and hunger strikes. [7] Prisoners in Turkey are holding an Italian in hopes of a swap. [8] The Kurds join Cyprus as contentious issues as the Turkish President goes to Vienna to discuss European Union membership.
