[1] The 1998 election ended the political career of Newt Gingrich, who had become Speaker in 1994, leading a conservative, issue-driven, GOP majority. [2] In Oct Democrats were urged to go after him. [3] Although both houses retained a narrowed GOP majority, Newt, with his combativeness and Impeachment-fixation, was blamed for the failures by both wings of his party. [4] The 1998 winners were Gore, Clinton, and moderates. [5] Newt's issues seemed dead in the booming economy and old Democratic loyalists ignored Clinton's morals and returned to the fold. [6] As fellow Republicans jockeyed for his positions, Newt resigned as Speaker on Nov 6 and also decided to leave his House seat.
