[1] Newspaper photos of the bruised face of Thailand's jailed former deputy minister, Anwar Ibrahim, has aroused international condemnation as well as local demonstrations. [2] Ibrahim, considered heir-apparent to Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamed, was fired by Mahathir for being morally unfit for office. [3] Anwar mounted a nationwide campaign against Mahathir before his arrest. [4] Mahathir served as Prime Minister for 17 years with a bold socio-economic program that had veered into recession. [5] Anwar differed with Mahathir over economic policy and began speaking out against him. [6] Mahathir has not selected a successor and if he resigns or dies, a power struggle could ensue.
